Update: HCC Coleman Wayfinding Monument

After more than a year since the Public Relations Department unvieled the HCC Coleman Wayfinding Monument, we decided it was time to update the design. By colaborating with Seebridge Media so early during the initial design phase, we were able to develop a system that would allow updates to be done with as few headaches as possible. Each image is printed on a piece of foamboard. All we would have to do is replace the foamboard pieces to refresh the look of the monument.

A few things had changed since we first unvieled the monument. First, we recieved a lot of real-world feedback, especially concerning the size of the fonts. The text on the monument was never meant to be read from far away, however, we did feel that the size and color of the headlines prevented people from being able to see from a reasonable distance exactly what information the monument offered. Also, Houston Community College has had a shift in design philosophy, so there was a need to simplify and align the design with district standards.

The biggest change, however, was the fact that our department no longer had a graphic designer. It would be up to me to design this piece on my own.

I began the design process by studying images of environmental design displays our department had previously collected and added a few of my own discoveries. A trend I noticed and wanted to bring to this update was containing each bit of information within a singular space. Our initial design included images that flowed between blocks, and this created a lot of headaches when assembling the display. I also decided to cut the amount of information on the design and only showcase directional informaiton.

I developed several different layout patterns for the front of the design, which would focus soley on wayfinding information. I showed the design to HCC Coleman's president who selected Idea #1. We both agreed that we liked the way the white, directional signs were situated across the design instead of being crushed all together.

I decided to go in a different direction for the back. Occassionally, the display has to be moved to make room for events in its usual first floor location. During these occassions, one side of the display would be visible showcasing information that didn't really make sense in that context. I wanted to create something that would honor our students and would make sense no matter the context.

The solution I came up with is "Faces of Coleman." Each block will feature a black and white photo of one of our students. This will allow us to showcase the enthusiasm and diversity of our students.

I am currently in the process of refining the wayfinding portion of the monument design. I want to ensure that each part fits perfectly and is readable. I am making plans for how to recruit students for the "Faces of Coleman" photo project.

HCC Coleman Program Card: Student Services Handout

The HCC Coleman Student Services department needed a way to showcase all of the programs the college offers. Previously, the department had handed out copies of our academic catalog The Book but with the colling going back to printing program-specific brochures using The Book became redundant.

To simply things, we decided it would be best to develop a card that listed out all of our programs. I also decided to include a paragraph that gave a general outline of HCC Coleman and its programs.

For the reverse side of the card, I wanted to showcase the variety of programs HCC Coleman offers to students. I arranged each square program photo in a grid to show that although there are a variety of programs at our college, each program helps create a strong college as a whole.

The design was turned into student services who approved of the design direction. We are currently working on getting the card printed and delivered to our college.

HCC Coleman Brochure: New Brochure Template

For the previous three years, HCC Coleman didn't publish brochures. Instead we've relied on our academic catalog The Book to inform students on how to apply to our programs. With a change in college administration, it was decided that it was time to publish brochures once again.

I took this opportunity to reevaluate the standard design of Houston Community College's brochures, which were packed with so much copy as to make them hard to navigate. Plus, a brochure should provide only a taste of what an organization has to offer. It should help direct students towards the college website or be used as a conversation starter with an academic advisor.

I simplified the design and added a circle to the front to break up the blockiness of previous brochure designs. Each brochure featuers a large program photo and the program title.

For the inside of the brochrue, I deleted any information I deemed unneccesary or that could be easily axcessed by visiting our website. I also worked to ensure there was plenty of spacing between lines and sections to make the copy easier to read and understand.

Reaction to this new brochure design has been very positive. The head of HCC's Communications department has declared my design the new standard template for all brochures going forward.
